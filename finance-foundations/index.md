## Theories

- [Black-Scholes 1973](theories/01-black-scholes-1973/README.md) - Black-Scholes prices European options from spot price, strike, time, rates, and volatility.
- [Market Efficiency Fama 1970](theories/02-market-efficiency-fama-1970/README.md) - Market efficiency says prices tend to incorporate available information, making consistent outperformance difficult.
- [Modern Portfolio Theory Markowitz 1952](theories/03-modern-portfolio-theory-markowitz-1952/README.md) - MPT says portfolio risk depends on weights, volatility, and correlation, not just individual asset risk.
- [CAPM Sharpe](theories/04-capm-sharpe/README.md) - CAPM links expected return to systematic market risk measured by beta.
- [Arbitrage Pricing Theory Ross 1976](theories/05-arbitrage-pricing-theory-ross-1976/README.md) - APT models expected return as compensation for exposure to multiple risk factors.
- [Agency Theory Jensen Meckling 1976](theories/06-agency-theory-jensen-meckling-1976/README.md) - Agency Theory explains conflicts between managers who control firms and shareholders who own them.
- [Limits to Arbitrage Shleifer Vishny 1997](theories/07-limits-to-arbitrage-shleifer-vishny-1997/README.md) - Limits to Arbitrage explains why mispricings can persist even when rational traders see them.
