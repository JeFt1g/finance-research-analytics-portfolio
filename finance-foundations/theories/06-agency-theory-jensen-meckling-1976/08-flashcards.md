# Agency Theory Jensen Meckling 1976 - Flashcards

## One-Sentence Summary

Agency Theory explains conflicts between managers who control firms and shareholders who own them.

## Why It Matters

It connects finance to incentives, governance, executive compensation, and real company behavior.

## Core Idea

Shareholders want long-term firm value. Managers may also want compensation, status, lower effort, empire building, or lower personal risk. Agency Theory studies that conflict and the mechanisms used to reduce it: monitoring, boards, ownership, audits, and incentive contracts.

## Key Terms

- Principal-agent problem
- Principal
- Agent
- Moral hazard
- Monitoring costs
- Agency costs
- Executive compensation
- Board oversight

## Formula / Model

```txt
Agency cost = monitoring costs + bonding costs + residual loss
Governance risk = weak monitoring + poor incentives + low alignment
```

## Simple Example

A CEO may pursue an acquisition that makes the company larger even if the deal has poor expected shareholder returns.

## Real-World Use

Proxy review, compensation analysis, governance risk databases, activist research, and private company incentive design.

## Limitations

- Not every disagreement is bad faith.
- Good incentives can still produce bad outcomes if measurement is poor.
- Governance text is messy and context-dependent.
