# Finance Glossary

## One-Sentence Summary

A simple but precise glossary for the core finance terms used in this repo.

## Why It Matters

Shared vocabulary prevents formula mistakes and makes finance software easier to design.

## Core Idea

### Option

A contract whose value depends on an underlying asset.

### Call option

Right to buy an asset at a strike price.

### Put option

Right to sell an asset at a strike price.

### Strike price

The contract price used for option exercise.

### Volatility

How much returns vary over time.

### Implied volatility

Volatility implied by an observed option price.

### Risk-free rate

The theoretical return over a period without default risk.

### Efficient market

A market where available information is quickly reflected in prices.

### Alpha

Return above what a risk model predicts.

### Beta

Sensitivity to market returns.

### Diversification

Combining assets whose risks do not fully overlap.

### Portfolio variance

Total portfolio return variability from weights and covariances.

### Correlation

How closely two return series move together.

### Systematic risk

Market-wide risk that diversification cannot remove.

### Idiosyncratic risk

Asset-specific risk that diversification can reduce.

### Arbitrage

A trade seeking profit from inconsistent pricing of similar exposure.

### Factor model

A model explaining returns through common risk drivers.

### Principal-agent problem

Conflict when one party acts for another with different incentives.

### Moral hazard

Risk-taking or low effort because the actor does not bear full cost.

### Mispricing

A gap between market price and estimated fair value.

### Liquidity

Ability to trade without large delay or price impact.

### Leverage

Borrowing or derivatives used to amplify exposure.

### Drawdown

Decline from a prior peak.

### Cost of capital

Required return demanded by capital providers.
