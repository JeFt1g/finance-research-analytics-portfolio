# Stock Valuation Case Study

## Problem

Value a Tesla-style sample company using a DCF model, comparable company analysis, and bull/base/bear scenarios.

## DCF Method

The model forecasts revenue, operating income, NOPAT, reinvestment, free cash flow, terminal value, enterprise value, equity value, and implied share price.

## Comparable Company Analysis

The project calculates peer EV/revenue, EV/EBITDA, and P/E multiples, then maps peer median valuation to an implied share price.

## Valuation Range

Bull, base, and bear assumptions create a valuation range and sensitivity table around discount rate and terminal growth.

## Risks

The largest risks are terminal value sensitivity, peer selection, growth assumptions, margin assumptions, and capital structure simplification.

## Final Recommendation

The report labels the sample company as undervalued, fairly valued, or overvalued based on base-case upside/downside.

## Real-World Use

The same structure can become a public-equity valuation memo once real filings, analyst estimates, and peer data are added.
